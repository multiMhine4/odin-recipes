Chongqing Cold Noodles

Home

Description

Chongqing cold noodles is a staple streetfood in the Chongqing region of China. It's cold noodles tossed in a sichuan dressing with spices, herbs, and possibly topped with other sichuan snacks.

Ingredients

  • 150g fresh noodles
  • Garnishes to taste
    • Roasted peanuts
    • Chilli oil (homemade or Lao Gan Ma)
    • Spring onion, sliced thinly
  • Sauce for 150g noodles
    • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon Chinese black vinegar
    • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
    • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
    • 1 tablespoon Chinese pickled vegetable (optional)

Steps

  1. Mix sauce ingredients together in a small dish.
  2. Cook the fresh noodles in 150g in boiling water for 2-3 minutes depending on thickness of noodle. Be sure to see bubbles occurring in pot, the rookie mistake is to throw noodles in before seeing bubbles.
  3. Once cooked, put under cold water in a strainer to prevent over cooking, this will retain the nice “bite” texture.
  4. Cover and mix noodles with the sauce.
  5. Garnish with spring onion and roasted peanuts to add a contrast in textures. Add chilli oil - you can either make your own or get “lao gan ma” from Asian grocer.
